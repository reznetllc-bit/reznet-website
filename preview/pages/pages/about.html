The RezNet Standard

Certification is available — and it means something.

“Certified” should not be a sticker. It should be a defensible claim based on measured conditions and documented outcomes. RezNet certification is awarded when a network meets defined requirements for stability, coverage, and readiness.

What we measure

Coverage & stability by area, interference/channel health, wired readiness, topology risk, and bottlenecks (ISP vs router vs Wi-Fi vs backhaul).

What certification means

The network meets the standard at the time of verification, and the homeowner has documentation for decisions, resale, or future upgrades.

What it does NOT mean

It does not guarantee an ISP’s external reliability, nor does it replace future changes in usage, layout, or new devices.

Certification levels

RezNet Verified

Baseline evaluation completed. Verdict issued. Risks identified. Roadmap provided. (This is what every assessment delivers.)

RezNet Certified

Network meets stability + coverage requirements with a clean, explainable topology. Certification is documented and issued after verification.

RezNet Certified+

Certified plus higher resilience: segmentation/hardening where appropriate (guest/IoT/work), reliability-first camera/IoT posture, and future-proofing notes.

Certification is always evidence-based. If the network doesn’t meet the standard yet, the report tells you exactly what to change and why.

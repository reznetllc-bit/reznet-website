Home Types & Real Limits

Most network advice ignores the biggest variable: your home. RezNet plans around structure, not wishful thinking.

The truths

Wi-Fi doesn’t “push through” structure

Dense materials, foil-backed insulation, brick, and HVAC can break beautiful lab results.

Speed ≠ stability

A fast speed test can coexist with drops, jitter, and “random” failures.

Backhaul decides everything

If your backhaul is weak, no number of nodes creates real reliability.

Empowerment

RezNet’s job is to give you a plan that matches reality — so you can stop blaming yourself, stop blaming the ISP blindly, and stop buying equipment as a coping mechanism.

Common home profiles

Older homes (plaster/lathe, additions)
  • Signal is unpredictable room-to-room
  • Wiring may be incomplete or inconsistent
  • Layout requires strategic AP placement
  • Often benefits from targeted wiring for backhaul
New builds (great on paper, often unfinished)
  • Pre-wire may exist but not terminated or labeled
  • Closets may be poorly ventilated or poorly located
  • Builder network “packages” prioritize cost over design
  • Perfect scenario for RezNet verification + roadmap
Multi-story (coverage is geometry)
  • Vertical coverage is not guaranteed
  • Stairwells and mechanical chases matter
  • Placement beats power
  • Backhaul makes multi-floor stable
Large footprints / long runs
  • “One router” is rarely correct
  • Mesh can work, but only with correct backhaul
  • Edge areas need measured planning
  • RezNet prevents overbuilding and underbuilding

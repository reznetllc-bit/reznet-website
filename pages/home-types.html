Common house structures

Here’s what typically breaks networks — and how RezNet approaches each situation.

Older plaster / lath / dense walls

Thick walls can crush signal. “More power” often makes things worse.

  • Fix is placement + more APs
  • Sometimes needs cable runs
  • We prioritize stability over speed hype
Brick / stone / fireplace cores

Masonry and chimney stacks can create hard dead zones.

  • APs must be staged around obstacles
  • Wired backhaul may be required
  • We map “shadow zones” clearly
Large / multi-story homes

One router won’t cover it. Floors kill signal faster than people expect.

  • Backhaul matters (wired > mesh)
  • Placement beats “bigger router”
  • We design for consistent experience
New builds with “structured wiring”

Often the wiring exists but it’s unfinished, unlabeled, or untested.

  • Closet layout determines success
  • Easy to become pro-grade quickly
  • We verify what’s real vs assumed
Homes with additions / garages / outbuildings

Additions are frequently built with materials that isolate Wi-Fi.

  • Outbuildings often need a wire
  • Point-to-point options exist
  • We explain cost vs reliability
High device density (smart home)

It’s not just speed — it’s airtime, stability, and interference.

  • We reduce congestion with design
  • We segment devices when needed
  • We keep it homeowner-friendly

Limitations we call out (so you don’t waste money)

“One router will do it”

Usually false for multi-story or >2,000 sq ft. We prove it with data.

Mesh without wired backhaul

Can be fine… or can cut performance hard. We test and recommend realistically.

Bad ISP modem/router placement

If the ISP drop is in the wrong corner, wiring or repositioning may be required.

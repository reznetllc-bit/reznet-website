Different houses have different network limits.

Wi-Fi is physics. Wiring is infrastructure. Layout is destiny. The fastest way to waste money is to ignore the structure of the home.

House types & common constraints

Brick / masonry-heavy
  • Signal attenuation is real (especially 5GHz)
  • “One router” rarely works across floors
  • Best fix is proper AP placement + wired backhaul
Two-story + long hallways
  • Roaming and overlap become the issue
  • Stability > raw throughput
  • Ceiling AP centerline usually beats “mesh in corners”
New builds (structured wiring)
  • Often un-terminated or mispatched
  • Closet readiness decides everything
  • Once wiring is correct, the rest gets easy
Limitations & truths

What Wi-Fi cannot do

Fix bad backhaul

If the “mesh node” is feeding on weak signal, it will amplify the problem — not solve it.

Ignore materials

Brick, tile, mirrors, fireplaces, metal ducting, radiant barriers — all impact coverage and stability.

Replace wiring

When wiring is possible, wired backhaul is the most reliable upgrade you can buy.
